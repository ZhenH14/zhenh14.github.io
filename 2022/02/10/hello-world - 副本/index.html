Just Random stuff.

Thinking back to a Korean movie I really enjoyed called “The Man Standing Next,” there’s this scene where the United States pushes South Korea to transition to democracy ASAP. The then-President, Park Chung-hee, says that the “national quality” of Koreans isn’t like that of the U.S., and the country isn’t ready for such a transition yet. This reasoning always gives me a sense of déjà vu.

The notion of societal quality and the clash due to unique historical or cultural factors has long been a popular argument to explain the failure of democracy in certain countries or predict its unlikelihood. Of course, both arguments hold some truth. Democratic nations didn’t outnumber authoritarian ones until around the millennium, might sound counterintuitive. But for the vast majority of Chinese people, democracy was an unheard-of concept 30 years ago, much like explaining space travel to ancient humans. And, under the prolonged reign of authoritarian and monarchical systems, democracy can spark severe resistance. This is because both authoritarian and monarchical systems share power monopolies, whereas democracy is about power competition; the former’s struggles tend to be ruthless, while the latter’s involve more concessions. Winners of the former expect long-term rule, while the latter, though entertaining similar ideas, often succumb to the influence of different power players.

It’s hard to definitively say whether democracy is the best choice, but a mainstream trend emerges from history: the shift from ancient monarchs centralizing power to modern-day decentralization. Though the degree of this decentralization may vary slightly, if we consider monarchy as the pivotal point, humanity has indeed been progressing toward dispersing power among multiple interest groups. The core isn’t just mechanically splitting power; it’s about effectively engaging each group’s desire for control.

想起以前看一部很喜欢的韩国电影“南山的部长们”。里面有个桥段：美国要求韩国尽快进行民主转型，时任总统朴正熙说，韩国人的集体素质不像美国，所以还不适合现在转型。这个理由总让我有些似曾相识之感。

民众素质和历史文化导致的水土不服，一直是很流行的论调，以解释民主体制在某一国的失败，或预测在某一国的不可能。当然，两者都有一定道理。前者，民主体制国家一直到千禧年左右才在数量上超越威权国家，虽然听起来很反直觉，但是民主体制对于绝大多数中国人来说，在30年前还是闻所未闻的事物，一如向古代人描绘宇宙飞行。后者，在威权国家和君主制的长期统治下，民主会带来极其严重的排斥效应，这是因为威权和君主制的共同点是权力垄断，而后者则是权力竞争；前者的斗争往往你死我活，后者的斗争以让步为主；前者获胜者期待长期统治，后者虽然有如此想法但碍于不同势力的政治参与而作罢。

很难说，民主是不是最好的选择，但是从历史上我们可以看到这样一个主流趋势：从古代君主的权力归于一极，到近现代权力的去中心化。当然，这种去中心化的程度略有不同，但是若以君主制作为轴标，人类的确在往多个利益集团分割权力的路上走着。其核心要素不在于机械地分割权力，而是充分调动每个集团的掌权欲：因此，不同集团有充分动机互相攻击，以此实现互相制约。当然，这并不意味着民主是最优选，因为它也可能是矫枉过正的，所以用历史趋势或潮流来论述其正当性并不强健。
